a simple exampLe to make your own minionhello world

Step1 - Make sure the client example works first.

Step2 - Create and edit a new .htm Click on the 'Copy to Clipboard' Button above and paste contents into your new .htm file.

Step3 - Important: Before saving your .htm file, change the follow sample values to valid ones for your identity and minion.

Replace sample Identity values with yours...

  nickname: Use a 'one word public name', that others will know you as. 

  member: Use a 'valid email address' (Note: must be different than email used in client example)

  namespace: a 'one word name' to isolate your minion from others. (many use their business name or last name').

  pin: a four digit or more number that is easy to remember.

Replace sample Minion values with yours...

  minionName: a one word name for your minion,

  minionType: a one word category for your minion (i.e. gif, form, ...),

  minionVersion: a number that indicates this minions version,

  description: a short description of your minions functionality,

Step4 - Save the changes you made to your .htm file. Your minion should now be ready to roll! Open your .htm file in your web browser (I use Chrome). Open the bowsers console and if all went well you should see your minion is registered , running, and waiting for clients to use your minion!

NOTE: To use your minion, you will need it's minionSku. Your minionSku is the combination of the following fields you changed in your .htm file: 
'namespace_minionName_minionType_minionVersion'.

  Using the example field values, the minionSku would be:
 'globalminions_hellowWorld_example_1.0'

Again, your minionSku is derived from the field values you used in your .htm file.

Step5 - To test your minion, change the minionSku in your working Client example with your minionSku. Save your change and run the Client example in a new web browser window. Now both your client and your minion should be talking to each other to display your minion's message. Monitor both browser console's activities.

WARNING: The members email used for the Client and Minion must not be the same.

Troubles? Double check the changes you made for syntax or spelling issues. Look at your browser's console looking for any errors logged there. Or talk to us at our forum, so we can personally help out. https://minionlogic.freeforums.net/
